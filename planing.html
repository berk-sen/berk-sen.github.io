Importance of City Planning Against Flooding

Problems:

      1-) Possibility of mixing toxic substances into water and mains water.

      2-) Damage to public property and its historical fabric.

      3-) Disruption of traffic and daily imperatives (especially in the stock market)

      4-) Loss of life and property.

      5-) Traffic Jam


Solutions

1. In risky areas, waste water channels should be placed at certain intervals on the road lines so that they are painted as usual, and for monitoring, a signal transmitter should be placed on the main pipe at certain intervals and communicate with the relevant authorities in cases where the occupancy is above normal.

2. Auxiliary wastewater channels should be built or the volume of the main water wastewater pipe should be increased, especially in case of rain that falls more than the volume of the main pipeline can take in a unit of time in residential areas with a high risk.

3. We can put floors and sewage channels and a deflector around historic buildings that absorb water and prevent them from being damaged in a possible flood.

4. With NASA data, soil moisture should be checked frequently, when precipitation is detected, the soil should be dried or textiles (e.g. clay) that absorb water in it should be increased.

5. Part of the walls of underpasses located in risky areas within the city should be covered with water-absorbing material and the bottom part should consist of pipes with large sensors

6. Installation of shelters to protect the tunnels from flooding and Widening roads near risk zones.

7. Identify the risk factor (when reaching the city) and determine the estimated path line (NASA satellite data will be very helpful in this regard), change the direction of the water with deflector stones and reduce its force.

8. Creating alternative road maps that can block roads in these areas in a risky situation using NASA's soil moisture map.
